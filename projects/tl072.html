SIMPLE TL072

OPERATIONAL AMPLIFIERS

Opamps are an integrated circuit with an input, an inverting input and an output. The chip itself contains an amplifier with a huge internal gain, producing an output equal to the difference between voltage at the two inputs multiplied by that gain. By feeding part of the output back to one of the inputs, the chip can be used to amplify the signal at the other input. The gain of the amplifier circuit is determined by how much of the signal is fed back, with more feedback resulting in a smaller gain.

I have designed this circuit as a simple solution for managing audio levels within DIY effects pedals and circuit bent instruments. The simplicity makes it very easy to use alongside other circuits and the DC supply means that it can usually be powered from the same power supply as the instrument, toy or pedal you are modifying.

The 25k potentiometer will overload the amp and add a nice bit of distortion at higher amplifications, but this can be substituted for a smaller potentiometer to keep the signal clean. Similarly, the TL072 can also be substituted for other dual opamps like the LM358 or NE5532.
